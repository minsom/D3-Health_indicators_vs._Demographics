Associations Discovered Between Health Indicators and Demographics

Using the 2014 one-year estimates from the U.S. Census Bureau's American Community Survey, associations were discovered between health indicators and demographics.

  • Population lacking healthcare increases with poverty rate. Texas, Wyoming, and New Hampshire have higher percentages of people without healthcare compared to those states with similar poverty rates. Massachusetts has the highest rate with healthcare.
  • Considering age and household income, Texas and Georgia have higher populations lacking healthcare.
  • Smoking rate is positively associated with poverty, negatively with household income, but not affected by age. Utah has the lowest smoking rate.
  • Obesity has a weak positive association with poverty rate, a strong negative association with household income, but not much with age.
